Conference information

This page provides all information required for conference attendees.

It is currently under construction and to be finalised

Venues

The conference is hosted at the Blavatnik School of Government and Somerville College.

Talks and poster sessions are held at the Blavatnik. Please see the visitor information page for more information.

Quick links:

  • Blavatnik on Google Maps
  • Somerville College on Google Maps

Slack

The primary hub for organisation is the conference Slack workspace ProbGen22. You should receive an invite to this workspace via email in the days before the conference. If you have not received an invite or are having difficulty getting access, please email the organisers at probgen22@maillist.ox.ac.uk.

More information on how to best use the Slack workspace will be posted in the coming days.

Talks

Talks are 15 minutes long, with 5 minutes allowed for questions and changeover. Session chairs will be asked to keep strictly to schedule, so please do not overrun the alloted time.

All talks take place at the Blatvatnik, and are streamed online using Zoom. Zoom links will be emailed to delegates and provided in the Slack workspace.

Detailed information on preparing slides will be posted in the coming days.

Posters

Poster sessions will be held virtually on Slack, where each poster is presented in its own channel. We encourage authors to be available in the channel during their appointed session.

Even numbered poster IDs (as listed on the abstracts page) will be presented in the poster session on Monday, and odd numbered IDs on Tuesday.

An in-person poster reception will be held at the Blavatnik in the spacious Inamori Forum. We hope that delegates will bring their laptops along to this reception so that we can enjoy the best of both in-person and online interactions.

We ask poster presenters to prepare a poster summarising the research which can be displayed in their Slack channel. Additionally, presenters may consider preparing a short (~3 minute) video giving overview of the work which can also be pinned to the channel. This is a very effective way of getting your message across to as many people as possible.

Examples showing a suggested way of organising the poster channel will be added in the coming days.

Meals

Breakfast is included for delegates staying at Somerville college.

Lunch is not provided. Because there is no conference activity during the mornings, we hope that attendees will take the opportunity to explore Oxford, and to enjoy the many great food options available. Please see the Food and drink section for more information.

There will be regular tea and coffee breaks through the afternoon talk sessions.

Dinner is provided at Somerville college for all in-person delegates each evening.
